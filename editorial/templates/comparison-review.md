# comparison-review

## Use when

Use for an actual reader decision, not affiliate inventory.

## Required inputs

- An accepted `content-brief.json` naming the decision, audience, unique value, evidence requirements, and commercial relationship.
- An accepted `research-packet.json` with comparable claim-level evidence, counterevidence, uncertainty, and direct-test artifacts.
- Official documentation, current pricing, current terms, and changelogs for every compared product.

## Required public sections

1. Decision and audience.
2. Inclusion and exclusion criteria.
3. Tested versions, access and commercial relationships.
4. Comparable evidence table.
5. Findings by decision criterion.
6. Best fit and poor fit for each option.
7. Limitations.
8. Independent conclusion.
9. Affiliate disclosure when applicable.
10. Sources and update trigger.

The rendered article must also expose a concise summary, visible authorship, AI-assistance disclosure when applicable, related knowledge, and an update/correction area.

## Evidence gate

Official documentation, pricing, terms, and changelogs are required for each compared product. Evidence must be current, collected against the same disclosed criteria, and mapped in `research-packet.json`. A product may receive a `Tested` label only when direct testing evidence records its environment, version, date, and artifacts. Commercial access and relationships must be visible.

## Stop conditions

Stop when any option lacks the required official documentation, pricing, terms, or changelog evidence; criteria cannot be applied comparably; a `Tested` label lacks direct evidence; commercial relationships are unknown; or the conclusion is affiliate inventory rather than an independent reader decision. Preserve material disagreement and request editorial judgment.
