# test-report

## Use when

Use for original evidence.

## Required inputs

- An accepted `content-brief.json` containing the question, unique value, test requirement, versions, date, and reviewer role.
- An accepted `research-packet.json` separating observations, sourced facts, calculations, interpretations, and opinions.
- Immutable or reproducible test artifacts, logs, screenshots, and environment details.

## Required public sections

1. Question and hypothesis.
2. Environment and versions.
3. Procedure.
4. Observations and artifacts.
5. Results.
6. Failures and anomalies.
7. Limitations and reproducibility.
8. Conclusion bounded to the evidence.
9. Sources, corrections and retest trigger.

The rendered article must also expose a concise summary, visible authorship, AI-assistance disclosure when applicable, related knowledge, and commercial relationships.

## Evidence gate

Each result and conclusion must trace to a dated artifact or sourced fact. The environment, versions, procedure, observations, failures, anomalies, counterevidence, and uncertainty must be recorded well enough for reproduction, and the conclusion may not extend beyond the observed evidence.

## Stop conditions

Stop when environment or version data is missing, artifacts cannot be preserved, the procedure is not reproducible, observations cannot be separated from interpretation, contradictory results are omitted, or a failed reproduction would require hiding or broadening the evidence. Record failure honestly and narrow or reject the conclusion.
